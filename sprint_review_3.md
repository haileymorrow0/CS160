# Changelog - Checkly Sprint #3 Update

## Hailey Thompson
## March 27, 2026

## [Sprint 3]

### Sprint Goal
Create a high quality version of Checkly that reflects Sprint 2 feedback and presents a more polished, realistic version of the app's final design.

### Increment
For this increment, I focused on turning Checkly from a functional layout into a more realistic product experience. This included improving the visual design, refining screen structure, and making the wireframe closer to what users would expect in a real finished app.

### Changelog
- **High-Fidelity Dashboard Design:** Upgraded the dashboard into a polished version with nicer visuals.
- **Improved Category Experience:** Updated category tabs and list organization so navigation feels more intuitive and easier to flow through.
- **Kid Mode Concept Screen:** Designed a simplified Kid Mode screen with larger text, cleaner navigation, and a more focused layout for kids
- **Visual Consistency:** Applied more consistent colors, icons, and interface patterns across screens so the app feels more consistent.

### Feedback Received
- My kids thought it looked nice.
- Stakeholders said the app looked much more polished and easier to understand than earlier drafts.
- The updated dashboard layout and category tabs made the app easier to navigate.
- Shared list permissions should be made more obvious so users can quickly tell who can edit and who can only view.
- Kid Mode should stay simple and visually distinct from the main app experience.

### Work in Progress
- Continuing to refine the shared list experience, especially around invitations and permission labels.
- Planning how search, reminders, and due dates will fit into the polished wireframe in a future sprint.
- Using stakeholder feedback to guide the next round of design and backlog priorities.

### Project Management
- **Tracking Tool:** [Jira](https://byupathway-team-j753k3ol.atlassian.net/jira/software/projects/DEV/boards/1/backlog)
- **Current Velocity:** Stable; on track to transition into the next sprint with clearer design direction.
