# Changelog - Checkly Sprint #4 Update

## Hailey Thompson
## April 3, 2026

## [Sprint 4]

### Sprint Goal
Finalize the collaborative "Shared List" logic by implementing clear permission labels and adding a safety layer to the Kid Mode experience.

### Increment
For this increment, I shifted focus from general design to specific user interaction and safety. This meant building out the "logic" of how two people share a list and ensuring that the Kid Mode isn't just a different look, but a protected space that stays simple and functional for kids.

### Changelog
- **Permission Identity System:** Implemented clear "Editor" and "Viewer" status tags within shared lists to resolve previous confusion about user roles.
- **Enhanced Kid Mode Logic:** Added a "Parental Lock" concept (PIN entry) to prevent kids from accidentally navigating into the main app settings or deleting shared tasks.
- **Refined Invitation UI:** Streamlined the process of inviting a family member to a list with a more prominent "Share" button and a simplified link-generation flow.
- **Kanban Workflow Optimization:** Successfully integrated **WIP (Work-in-Progress) Limits** on the Jira board to prevent task overload and identify bottlenecks in the design-to-development pipeline.

### Feedback Received
- My "Scrum Consultant" (husband) noted that the "Owner" status should be visually distinct from "Editor" to show who has billing/deletion rights.
- Stakeholders (also husband) appreciated the PIN lock on Kid Mode, saying it makes the app feel more "family-ready."
- The new WIP limits on the board were praised for making the "In Progress" column less cluttered.
- Future feedback suggested looking into how "Reminders" will pop up on a lock screen when the app is closed.

### Work in Progress
- Finalizing the notification triggers for "Smart Reminders."
- Developing the "Personal Dashboard" to show productivity trends (tasks completed vs. missed).
- Exploring the "Cross-Device Sync" stability to ensure shared lists update instantly for both users.
